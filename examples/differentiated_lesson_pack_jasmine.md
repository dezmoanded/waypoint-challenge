# Differentiated Lesson Pack
**Lesson:** What is 'community' and why is it important? (Toby Lowe)
**Unit:** Unit 1: Community and Belonging | **Grade:** 7 | **Standard:** RI.7.2
**Student:** Jasmine | **Prepared:** 2026-05-05

---

## 1. Brief Overview

This lesson asks students to read a grade-level informational text, track the author's central idea across 11+ paragraphs, answer literal and inferential questions, and write a short response using text evidence and unit vocabulary. The standard (RI.7.2) is preserved at grade level.

**Jasmine's most relevant IEP needs for this lesson:**
- Reading at approximately 3rd grade level (Informational Text Comprehension: Grade 2 on iReady Fall 2025); struggles with literal and inferential comprehension verbally and in writing
- Low reading stamina and writing stamina; avoidance behaviors (head down, restroom requests) emerge when tasks feel too hard
- Difficulty with task initiation; benefits from "pause, plan, proceed" reminders
- Requires graphic organizers, checklists, reference sheets, and copy of teacher notes (IEP: Accommodations and Modifications)
- Performs significantly better in small group or 1:1 settings; enrolled in daily 55-minute co-taught ELA (IEP: Service Delivery)
- ELA annual goal: increase text comprehension and writing responses from 50% to 75% accuracy (IEP: Measurable Annual Goals, Goal 3)

**Main differentiation approach:** Chunk the text and task demands into smaller, labeled pieces; provide pre-filled scaffolds so Jasmine is completing thinking rather than generating organization; build in a predictable check-in pattern so frustration is caught before shutdown; keep the intellectual goal (central idea + evidence) fully intact.

## 3. Modified Lesson Flow

### Phase 1: Intro Slide Deck (5 minutes)

**What to do:** Before the slide deck begins, place Jasmine's pre-printed "anchor card" on her desk. The card has: (1) today's reading skill in one sentence — *"Today's skill: Find the central idea and the details that develop it"* — and (2) the lesson's key question — *"What does Lowe say a community is?"* After the slide deck, say: *"We'll be coming back to these two things all period."*

**Why it helps:** Jasmine's IEP notes difficulty with task initiation and benefit from repeated directions (Accommodations and Modifications). The anchor card externalizes the cognitive task of remembering the lesson goal so attention can go to the text.

**Materials needed:** Pre-printed anchor card (half-sheet, large font).

**Student-facing language:** *"Keep this on your desk — we'll use it all class."*

**Accommodation reminder:** Sit Jasmine in front; co-teacher seats nearby.

---

### Phase 2: Vocabulary Preview (integrated into intro, 3–4 minutes)

**What to do:** Hand Jasmine her 4-box vocab card. For each term, say the word, point to the pronunciation, give a one-sentence example using a community she knows (e.g., a school sports team or neighborhood). Ask her to write or say one word that comes to mind. Do not require full sentences.

**Why it helps:** Vocabulary at Grade 3 (iReady); pre-teaching with concrete examples reduces the cognitive load of encountering unknown words mid-reading, directly supporting ELA Goal 3 (comprehension from 50% → 75%).

**Materials needed:** 4-box vocab card (term / pronunciation / simple definition / "my example" blank).

**Student-facing language:** *"Before we read, let's make sure these four words are familiar. You just need one example — it can be a word, a picture, or a sentence."*

---

### Phase 3: During Reading — Whole Class, Paragraphs 1–2 (approx. 10 minutes)

**What to do:**
1. Paragraph 1 is highlighted on Jasmine's copy. After reading, co-teacher points to Q1 on her scaffold sheet and gives the sentence starter.
2. For Q2, prompt: *"Look at your two boxes. What did the list add to the claim?"* Pause for 30 seconds before calling on the class.
3. For Q3, prompt Jasmine to circle the traits in the bullet list first, then copy up to 3. Check her work before partner reading begins.

**Why it helps:** The scaffolds address literal and inferential comprehension barriers documented in Present Levels. Circling before writing reduces the writing-stamina demand at this early stage.

**Materials needed:** Scaffold sheet (Q1–Q3), highlighting already done on Jasmine's text copy.

**Student-facing language:** *"Circle the traits you find — that counts as your answer. Then we'll write them."*

**Accommodation reminder:** Repeated directions; co-teacher re-states each sub-task.

---

### Phase 4: During Reading — Partner Reading, Paragraphs 3–7 (approx. 10 minutes)

**What to do:**
1. Assign Jasmine's partner intentionally (reliable, patient reader).
2. Partners alternate reading paragraphs aloud.
3. For Q4, Jasmine uses the two-column scaffold with the word bank. Co-teacher circulates and checks in at the midpoint.
4. For Q5 (personal community), Jasmine may draw her community symbol rather than write the name. Pair records both responses on the half-sheet.

**Why it helps:** Partner reading reduces independent reading-stamina demand. The word bank for Q4 gives Jasmine the vocabulary of the 6 traits without requiring recall, keeping the inference demand intact. IEP Behavioral/Social-Emotional notes she enjoys peer interaction and performs better in small-group settings.

**Materials needed:** Scaffold sheet (Q4–Q5), word bank of 6 traits from paragraph 2.

**Student-facing language:** *"You and your partner are going to figure this out together. Column 1: what Lowe does. Column 2: which trait from our list matches."*

---

### Phase 5: During Reading — Whole Class, Paragraphs 8–11 (approx. 10 minutes)

**What to do:**
1. Before reading, point to the pre-printed margin labels on Jasmine's copy: "List 1: traits" and "List 2: test."
2. After reading, give the two-blank scaffold for Q6 (comparing lists). Wait for Jasmine to fill blank 1 before moving on.
3. For Q7, co-teacher checks in after Sentence Frame 1 is complete. If Jasmine is stuck, offer to scribe her spoken answer.
4. For Q8, give the verbal "read one paragraph, then stop" prompt. Check in between the two highlights.

**Why it helps:** Tracking two non-contiguous text locations is a high-load demand for a student with low informational comprehension (Grade 2, iReady). Margin labels and sequential prompting directly address this. Scribing preserves the intellectual demand (identifying evidence) while removing the writing-stamina barrier, consistent with IEP writing supports.

**Materials needed:** Scaffold sheet (Q6–Q8), Jasmine's pre-labeled text copy.

**Student-facing language:** *"You already know what List 1 is from earlier. Now find the match in the second list."*

**Accommodation reminder:** Co-teacher offers to scribe spoken answers for Q7 if stamina is flagging.

---

### Phase 6: Independent Practice (20 minutes, including break)

**What to do:**
1. Signal a 2-minute movement break before independent practice begins (stand, stretch, brief walk to water fountain if permitted). This is a scheduled break per IEP Accommodations.
2. Read the multiple-choice question aloud to Jasmine. She selects her answer and may refer to her scaffold sheet.
3. Hand Jasmine the 3-part short response organizer (see Section 4). Explain: *"Part 1 first, then I'll check in."*
4. Co-teacher checks in after Part 1 (definition in own words), then again after Part 2 (first text detail), before Jasmine completes Part 3.
5. Provide extended time per IEP accommodation.

**Why it helps:** The scheduled break prevents the end-of-lesson stamina crash that triggers avoidance behaviors (head down, restroom requests), documented in Present Levels: Behavioral. The 3-part organizer converts a high-load open writing task into a sequenced fill-in, directly targeting ELA Goal 3 and the graphic organizer accommodation listed in IEP Assessments (A9).

**Materials needed:** 3-part short response organizer, anchor card, scaffold sheet for reference.

**Student-facing language:** *"Take your break. When you come back, start with Part 1 only — just one sentence."*

**Accommodation reminder:** Extra time; graphic organizer permitted; small group space available if Jasmine requests.

---

### Phase 7: Student-Led Discussion (5 minutes)

**What to do:**
1. Thirty seconds before partners begin, co-teacher reads Question 1 quietly to Jasmine and asks: *"What community are you thinking of?"* Give her one answer before the timer starts.
2. Jasmine records her answer on her half-sheet (labeled "Me:"), then asks her partner and records ("Partner:").
3. If time allows, move to Question 2. Question 3 is optional/extension.

**Why it helps:** Pre-seeding one answer removes the task-initiation barrier for discussion (documented in Present Levels: Academics). The half-sheet structure means Jasmine has a clear, concrete task (fill in two lines per question) rather than an open-ended oral exchange, reducing anxiety and increasing participation.

**Materials needed:** Discussion half-sheet (3 questions, Me/Partner lines).

**Student-facing language:** *"You've already got an answer for Question 1 — now it's your turn to share it with your partner."*

### Short Response — 3-Part Organizer

**Prompt:** *Explain what Lowe means when he says a community is "a group of people who share an identity-forming narrative."*

**Part 1 — Put the definition in your own words (1–2 sentences):**
> Lowe means that a community is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_.

**Part 2 — Text detail #1 (copy or paraphrase a sentence from the text + paragraph number):**
> One detail that supports this is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (paragraph \_\_).

**Part 3 — Text detail #2 + one unit vocabulary word:**
> Another detail is \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ (paragraph \_\_).
> This connects to the word *[narrative / aspect / moral / specific]* because \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_.

## 5. Assessment and Output Adjustments

**Multiple Choice (RI.6):**
The question is read aloud by the co-teacher. Jasmine may refer to her scaffold sheet and anchor card. No modification to the question or answer choices is needed — reading aloud removes the reading-barrier without changing the task demand.

**Short Response (RI.7.2):**
The 3-part organizer preserves the full intellectual demand: Jasmine must interpret the phrase "identity-forming narrative" in her own words, select two pieces of text evidence, and use at least one unit vocabulary word. What changes is the structure — she is given the organizational frame so that her cognitive effort goes to comprehension and evidence selection, not paragraph organization. This directly targets ELA Goal 3 (text comprehension and writing responses from 50% → 75%) and the graphic organizer accommodation (IEP: Assessments, A9).

**Success criteria remain unchanged:**
- Fully answers the prompt ✓
- Includes at least two details from the text ✓
- Incorporates relevant unit vocabulary ✓

**Discussion:**
Jasmine records written responses on the half-sheet (see Section 4). This serves as documentation of her oral participation and reduces the cognitive load of holding partner answers in working memory. No reduction in question rigor.

**If Jasmine does not complete the short response in class:** Co-teacher uses the organizer as a talking scaffold during a 1:1 check-in (per daily 55-minute co-taught ELA service). Jasmine dictates; co-teacher scribes; Jasmine reviews and confirms.
